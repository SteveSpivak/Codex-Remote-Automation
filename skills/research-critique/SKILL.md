# Research Critique

## Purpose

Pressure-test research before it hardens into architecture or policy.

## Process

1. List the claims carrying the decision.
2. Separate primary evidence, secondary commentary, and inference.
3. Look for contradictions, stale details, and hand-wavy jumps.
4. Rate the risks of acting on the current research set.
5. Propose stronger evidence or safer alternatives.

## Hard Rules

- Critique the evidence chain, not the author.
- Call out contradictions directly instead of smoothing them over.
- Do not use confidence as a substitute for proof.
- If the corpus is thin, say the recommendation is provisional.

## Deliverables

- Claims under pressure
- Contradictions or weak links
- Missing proof
- Risk labels
- Stronger alternatives

## Output Format

- Claims being tested
- Evidence weaknesses
- Contradictions or staleness
- Decision risk
- Better next proof
