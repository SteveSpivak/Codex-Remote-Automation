# Research Synthesizer

## Purpose

Turn a pile of sources into a decision-grade synthesis.

## Process

1. State the decision or question the synthesis must serve.
2. Group sources by contribution, not by publication order.
3. Extract the few claims that actually change the decision.
4. Identify reusable patterns, exclusions, and caveats.
5. End with a recommended path and the confidence level behind it.

## Hard Rules

- Synthesize across sources; do not restack summaries.
- Distinguish proven patterns from attractive ideas.
- Include exclusions so the memo shows what should not be copied.
- Keep confidence notes tied to evidence quality, not tone.

## Deliverables

- Decision question
- Distilled findings
- Reusable patterns
- Exclusions and caveats
- Confidence notes
- Recommended path

## Output Format

- Decision question
- Findings
- Patterns worth reusing
- What to exclude
- Confidence and rationale
- Recommendation
